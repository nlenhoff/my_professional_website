Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

January 2020 - May 2022

As an undergraduate TA for the University of Maryland, College Park, I assisted with a course called ENEE150 - "Intermediate Programming Concepts for Engineers" for five consecutive semesters. This course focused on C programming concepting including pointers, memory management, multithreading, recursion, polymorphism, encapsulation, and best practices. My duties included teaching a weekly recitation section, holding office hours for students, grading, and running exam review sessions. I graded exams and assignments and participated in both preparing exam questions and proctoring exams. Each week I organized and planned my recitation curriculum and met with the professor and other TAs to manage grading and course pacing. I created and maintained a personal website to provide resources for the class and updated it on a weekly basis with lecture notes, videos, and code samples. During the Covid lockdown I recorded my lectures and posted them for students to view as able.

Teaching Ambassador

May 2019 - February 2021

Summer Camps and Afterschool Workshops
During the summer of 2019 I worked for the Iribe Initiative for Inclusion and Diversity in computing. For K-12 students, I created curriculum and projects for summer camps covering a variety of topics. One program focused on wearable technology and utilized Arduino, microcontrollers, embedded circuits, art, and sewing. Other camp programs covered Python programming website development (utilizing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript), and computer science topics incorporating data encryption, error detection, programming practices, and cybersecurity. At the conclusion of the summer started leading and assisting with outreach events and workshops on the University of Maryland, College Park campus. I began participating in the following programs. All programs were managed by the Iribe Initiative.

Elementary School Programs
As a part of the weekly afterschool "Creative Computing" program, I taught computer science concepts to elementary school students. My team and I developed curriculum for Scratch projects, cypher games, and Python turtle art activities.

Girl Scout Workshops
During the Covid lockdown I continued to participate in youth education by teaching online. I developed original curriculum for Girl Scout Workshops so the girls could earn badges in computer science and cybersecurity from home. Curriculum included creative activities, exercises, and stories to instill the knowledge of safe practices with technology and the internet.
