Undergraduate Research Assistant

August 2021 - May 2022

As an undergraduate research assistant, I worked on an ongoing research project run by the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of Maryland, College Park and partnered with CoolCAD, an electronics company focused on the design and production of semiconductor Silicon Carbide. The research was sponsored by DARPA to design a microprocessor with a low transistor count to endure harsh environments. I furthered the development by writing Verilog code for the microprocessor. My design work solidified the instruction set and finalized the state machine. During the implementation phase I debugged the existing codebase, implemented my designs, and created testbench simulations to prove out the instruction set and state machine. Finally, I ran the design on a Xilinx Artix FPGA and confirmed that the output data matched the testbench outputs when identical inputs were received. At the conclusion of my assistantship I presented my work to representatives from UMD and CoolCAD, and passed the project resources down to the next research assistant.
